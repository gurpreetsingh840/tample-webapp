Antim Ardas Ceremony

"A sacred ceremony of remembrance and prayer, offering peace to the departed soul and strength to the family."

The Antim Ardas Ceremony is the final prayer service held for a deceased individual. It is a solemn occasion that provides closure and honors the departed soul.

The Antim Ardas Ceremony is a time for family and friends to gather, reflect, and offer prayers for the peace of the departed soul. It is a time for remembrance, reflection, and spiritual support.

The ceremony includes the recitation of prayers, hymns, and the final Ardas (prayer). It is a time for the community to come together and offer their support and condolences to the grieving family.

The Antim Ardas Ceremony is a beautiful and meaningful tradition in Sikhism that provides comfort and solace to the grieving family and community. It is a time for prayer, reflection, and connection with the divine presence of the Guru.

The ceremony is often conducted in a Gurdwara or at the family's home, and it includes the recitation of the Guru Granth Sahib Ji and the performance of the final Ardas, offering prayers for the departed soul and comfort to the family.

Service Details

  • Final prayer ceremony
  • Reading of Guru Granth Sahib Ji
  • Ardas for departed soul
