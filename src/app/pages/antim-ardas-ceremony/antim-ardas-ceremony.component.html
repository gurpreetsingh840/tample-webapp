Antim Ardas Ceremony

The Antim Ardas Ceremony is the final prayer service held for a deceased individual. It is a solemn occasion that provides closure and honors the departed soul.

The Antim Ardas Ceremony is a time for family and friends to gather, reflect, and offer prayers for the peace of the departed soul. It is a time for remembrance, reflection, and spiritual support.

The Antim Ardas Ceremony is a beautiful and meaningful tradition in Sikhism that provides comfort and solace to the grieving family and community. It is a time for prayer, reflection, and connection with the divine presence of the Guru.

The Antim Ardas Ceremony is often conducted in a Gurdwara or at the family's home, and it includes the recitation of prayers, hymns, and the final Ardas (prayer). It is a time for the community to come together and offer their support and condolences to the grieving family.
