Akhand Path

Akhand Path is the continuous, front-to-back, reading of the Guru Granth Sahib, the Sikh Holy Scriptures. It is a sacred ritual that is often performed to mark significant occasions or to seek blessings.

The Akhand Path is a continuous, uninterrupted, non-stop recital of all the verses in the Guru Granth Sahib, the Sikh Holy Scriptures. It is a sacred and cherished tradition in Sikhism, often performed to mark significant occasions or to seek blessings.

The recitation takes approximately 48 hours and is carried out by a team of readers who take turns to ensure that the reading continues without any breaks. The Akhand Path is usually conducted in the presence of the congregation, who listen to the recitation and participate in the prayers.

The Akhand Path is a powerful spiritual practice that brings peace, harmony, and divine blessings to the participants and the community. It is a time for reflection, devotion, and connection with the Guru's teachings.

The Akhand Path is often performed to commemorate important events such as births, weddings, anniversaries, and other significant milestones. It is also conducted during times of hardship or to seek divine guidance and support.

The Akhand Path is a beautiful and profound expression of faith and devotion in Sikhism. It is a reminder of the eternal presence of the Guru and the timeless wisdom contained in the Guru Granth Sahib.

Ceremony Details

  • Complete recital of Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji
