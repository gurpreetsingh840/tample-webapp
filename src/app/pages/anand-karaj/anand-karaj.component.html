Anand Karaj

Anand Karaj is the Sikh marriage ceremony, which means "Blissful Union". It is a sacred and joyous occasion that unites two individuals in a spiritual and lifelong bond.

The Anand Karaj ceremony is a beautiful and meaningful event that is centered around the reading of the Laava, four hymns which formalize the marriage. The couple takes four rounds around the Guru Granth Sahib as the Laava are recited, symbolizing their commitment to each other and to the Guru's teachings.

The Anand Karaj ceremony is a joyous and festive occasion that is celebrated with family, friends, and the community. It is a time for prayer, reflection, and celebration of the couple's union.

The Anand Karaj ceremony is a sacred and cherished tradition in Sikhism that reflects the values of love, commitment, and spiritual connection. It is a beautiful expression of faith and devotion that brings the couple closer to the divine presence of the Guru.
