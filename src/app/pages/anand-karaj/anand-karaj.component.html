Anand Karaj

"The sacred union of two souls bound together in the presence of Guru Granth Sahib Ji."

Anand Karaj is the Sikh marriage ceremony, meaning "Blissful Union". The ceremony takes place in the presence of Guru Granth Sahib Ji. During the ceremony, the couple circles Guru Granth Sahib Ji four times while the Lavan (four hymns of marriage) are being read.

The Anand Karaj ceremony is a beautiful and sacred tradition in Sikhism that symbolizes the spiritual union of the couple. It is conducted in the presence of the Guru Granth Sahib Ji, the eternal Guru of the Sikhs.

The ceremony begins with the couple and their families gathering in the Gurudwara. The couple takes four rounds around the Guru Granth Sahib Ji, each round representing a vow of commitment and devotion to each other and to the Guru's teachings.

The Lavan hymns are recited during each round, and the couple is blessed by the congregation. The ceremony concludes with the final Ardas (prayer) and the distribution of Karah Prasad (sacred offering).

Anand Karaj is a joyous and spiritually uplifting ceremony that brings together the couple, their families, and the community in a celebration of love, faith, and commitment.
